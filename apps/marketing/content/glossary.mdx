# Glossary

This page defines the core terms used throughout OpenLoomi's documentation. These are the concepts that underpin how the product works.

---

## Connectors

A **Connector** is an integration between OpenLoomi and an external platform (Slack, Telegram, WhatsApp, Gmail, etc.). Connectors are what allow OpenLoomi to read and interact with your messages across different services.

See [Connectors](/docs/connectors) for the full list.

---

## Skills

**Skills** are specialized capabilities that OpenLoomi uses to accomplish specific tasks. OpenLoomi has access to skills and automatically selects the right ones based on what you ask. You can also manually select skills in the message composer.

See [Skills](/docs/skills) for the full list.

---

## Automation

An **Automation** is a workflow you create to automate repetitive tasks — such as sending scheduled summaries, responding to specific triggers, or routing messages into your OpenLoomi workspace.

See [Automation](/docs/automation) to get started.

---

## Attention Agent

The **Attention Agent** is the desktop companion in OpenLoomi — a 168×168, always-on-top, transparent little fox named Loomi with its own expression animations. It surfaces a small bubble in the moments that matter (an email that needs a reply, a meeting invite past its RSVP deadline, your 9 AM "today's to-do"), and stays quietly in the system tray even when you close the main window, waiting to come back with a single click.

See [Attention Agent](/docs/attention-agent) to learn more.

---

## Loop

**Loop** is OpenLoomi's proactive execution engine — it runs a pass every 10 minutes, picks out the signals scattered across your apps (email, meetings, PR comments, @mentions, Linear tickets), prioritizes them, and turns them into "decision cards" placed in front of you. You just glance at one and tap "Approve" — and Loop acts on your behalf. Every morning it delivers a "Today's To-Dos"; every evening, a "Today's Done".

See [Loop](/docs/loop) to learn more.
