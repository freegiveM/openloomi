# Founders & Business Development

# My Inbox Was Eating My Company

I'm a founder doing BD in a seed-stage company. Half my day is conversations — investor intros, partner pitches, customer follow-ups, advisor catch-ups. The other half of my day is supposed to be product, hiring, and runway. But by 6 PM, all I can remember is that I owe Sarah a reply, the partner from Helsinki never got the deck, and I was supposed to loop in my advisor three days ago.

I wasn't short on discipline. I was short on a way to hold 500 relationships in my head at the same time.

Then I tried OpenLoomi.

I didn't want another chatbot that waits for me to ask it something. I wanted an AI assistant that actually fits the founder's day: it watches the inbound continuously, organizes the context for me, and only finds me when there's a decision I genuinely need to make.

---

## Problem 1: I keep re-reading the same thread before every reply

When an investor I haven't talked to in three months writes back, the exchange used to go like this:

- Scroll back through our last 6 emails to remember what we discussed
- Check my notes for "what was the term sheet condition I said yes to"
- Re-read the reply twice because I don't want to contradict something I said in March
- Then write the reply

If I had three of these in a day, the half-hour tax on each one added up. If I had seven, I started dropping threads — not on purpose, just because the context-loading cost was higher than the embarrassment of missing the reply.

I realized this is exactly what AI should be doing. The actual hard part of replying to a past contact isn't the writing — it's gathering the scattered context and reorganizing it into something I can answer from.

So I connected Gmail and Slack in OpenLoomi:

```
use openloomi-connectors to connect Gmail
use openloomi-connectors to connect Slack
```

Verify with `list-accounts` — once both show `active`, Loop picks them up on the next tick.

Every 10 minutes, Loop polls both inboxes. When an inbound from a contact on my important list arrives with an action word — _"intro"_, _"follow-up"_, _"circle back"_, _"RSVP"_ — past my default 4-hour reply window, Loop surfaces an `email_reply` or `im_reply` decision card. The card carries:

- The **draft reply** — pre-written by the agent, grounded in what Sarah and I discussed last quarter
- The **reasoning trace** — _"Sarah is the investor who passed on Q1; she's circling back with new terms"_
- The **confidence score** (default range 0.60–0.85)
- The four-gesture flow: **Approve / Edit Draft / Later / Skip**

For Sarah's email about the Q3 deck, the card already had the draft referencing what I said in March. I glanced at the memory trace, tapped **Approve**, and moved on. Total time: 10 seconds.

## Problem 2: Cold outreach never sounds like me

For warm replies I had Loop. For cold outreach — first touch, follow-ups, re-engagements with contacts who went quiet three months ago — there was no incoming signal yet for Loop to surface.

The old approach was even worse. I'd open a doc of "templates" I'd collected from other founders, copy one, fill in the blanks, and send. The result always read like a template because it was a template. Replies came back at maybe 5%, and most of those were polite declines.

I started drafting first-touch in **Chat** with the agent. The agent reads my past messages and the relevant contact notes from `~/.openloomi/data/memory/people/`, so the draft sounds like the way I actually write, not like a Mad Libs form. The **`cold-email`** skill is the public packaging for this workflow; the **`revops`** skill helps me track lifecycle stages.

Try:

- _"Draft a follow-up to Sarah Chen based on our last conversation in March about Q3 terms."_
- _"Write a 5-touch follow-up sequence for the Acme intro — each touch should add new value, not just 'checking in.'"_
- _"Pull everything I know about Marcus from memory and draft a re-engagement email that references his move to Singapore."_

The agent reads the right slice of memory before it writes, drafts in my voice, and waits for me to refine and send. For a 5-touch sequence that used to take me 90 minutes of blank-page staring, it's now 20 minutes of editing.

## Problem 3: I find out about quiet contacts too late

The other category that fell through the cracks was re-engagement. A lead that went warm in January, an advisor I haven't looped in since Q1, a customer I promised to circle back to "after the launch" — all the relationships that should keep being touched but don't generate any signal in the inbox.

Loop handles this through `contact_update` cards. When a public signal lands — a promotion in their bio, a new role in their link-in-bio, a podcast they were just on — Loop surfaces a card with the proposed update to `people/<name>.md` in memory. I tap **Approve** and the contact profile gets refreshed; next time they email, the `email_reply` card carries the new context.

There's also a `digest` card Loop surfaces weekly with the contacts I've been quiet with for over 30 days. I open it, the agent drafts a one-line re-engagement in my voice for each, and I send the ones I want to keep warm. No "I forgot to reach out for half a year" anymore.

## What I really wanted to solve isn't a few automation tasks

At first I just wanted to save the half-hour per reply I was spending re-reading threads. But after a few weeks, I realized the real problem isn't that I'm missing a few automation scripts — it's that relationship work is fundamentally an information organization problem:

- Context lives in Gmail, Slack, Telegram, LinkedIn, Notion, my head
- The same person appears in five of those tools with five slightly different stories
- Follow-up signals are buried under 200 other messages
- Time-zone gaps and async work mean half of "yesterday" actually happened overnight

In the past, all this information had to be found and pieced together by me actively searching. The change OpenLoomi brings is inverting that relationship: instead of me constantly searching for context, AI keeps watching the conversations and comes to me with the full memory slice at the right moment.

- If an important contact writes back, the `email_reply` card lands with the draft and the memory trace ready.
- If a DM needs a reply, the `im_reply` card carries the channel context and a grounded draft.
- If a contact has been quiet, the weekly `digest` card surfaces them with a re-engagement draft waiting.
- If a public signal changes someone's context, the `contact_update` card proposes the memory refresh.

I only need to focus on the parts that genuinely need my judgement — the actual message content, the terms, the personal relationship.

## From "email triage" to a continuously running founder loop

To make this work, I configured two kinds of working patterns in OpenLoomi.

**One is event-driven:**

| Trigger                                                                | What happens                                                                     |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Inbound email from important contact + action word + past reply window | `email_reply` card surfaces with the draft grounded in memory; four-gesture flow |
| Inbound DM (Slack / Telegram / Lark / iMessage) needing reply          | `im_reply` card with the channel context and the draft                           |
| Public signal on a contact (promotion, new role, etc.)                 | `contact_update` card proposes the memory refresh                                |

**The other is scheduled:**

| Task                      | Schedule          | Purpose                                                         |
| ------------------------- | ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brief (Today's To-Do)** | Every day at 9 AM | Pulls today's calendar + open decision cards + quiet contacts   |
| **Wrap (Today's Done)**   | Every day at 6 PM | Lists the outreach Loop handled for you today                   |
| **Weekly Contact Digest** | Mondays at 9 AM   | Surfaces contacts quiet for > 30 days with re-engagement drafts |

Together they form a loop that runs continuously:

```
Continuously watch → Organize contact context → Propose draft or update → Human decides → Send and remember
```

Compared to configuring an isolated CRM workflow, I'd rather have OpenLoomi keep understanding _"who's in my network right now and which of them needs a touch today."_

> 💡 **Cold outreach isn't in this table** because it isn't a scheduled task and there's no incoming signal yet — it lives in Chat. Open the main OpenLoomi window, ask the agent to draft, refine, then send via the matching connector.

## How to set this up

The whole configuration isn't complicated. I just connect Gmail, Slack, and the messaging apps I actually use, in OpenLoomi Desktop, the Claude plugin, or the Codex plugin:

```
use openloomi-connectors to connect Gmail
use openloomi-connectors to connect Slack
use openloomi-connectors to connect Telegram
```

Gmail and Slack live in Composio's app catalog; the seven native Loomi connectors (Telegram / WhatsApp / iMessage / Feishu / DingTalk / QQ / WeChat) don't need Composio at all. You can mix native and Composio-managed accounts freely.

Once connected, I verify with `list-accounts`. When Gmail, Slack, and Telegram all show `active`, OpenLoomi picks them up on the next tick and starts the matching flows:

- Every 10 min, Loop polls each inbox and surfaces typed cards as signals clear the gates
- The Brief at 9 AM rolls up today's needs; the Wrap at 6 PM recaps what Loop handled
- Weekly digest surfaces the contacts I've been quiet with, with re-engagement drafts ready

For cold outreach (no incoming signal), I open **Chat** and ask the agent to draft. For per-source trust settings and brief/wrap times, I tune in **Settings → Loop**. Loomi's bubble cadence is tunable in **Settings → Attention Agent**.

OpenLoomi's audit log records every execution — how many times Loop has polled, which cards surfaced, which Approves I tapped, which drafts I edited. This way I can hand the repetitive triage off to AI and still see exactly what it did.

## What I need isn't another chatbot

This experience reinforced something for me: an AI assistant for a founder shouldn't be a chat window waiting for me to ask.

If every interaction still requires me to open a tool, type a question, and supply the context, then the responsibility for gathering information and pushing work forward still sits on my shoulders.

What I actually need is an AI that can keep working around my network:

- It watches the inboxes that matter — Gmail, Slack, Telegram, LinkedIn
- It keeps spotting the inbound that needs a reply
- It proactively organizes the contact context that's scattered across five tools
- It drafts the reply grounded in what I and the contact have already discussed
- It hands the send button back to me when judgement is needed
- It remembers my edits and choices, gradually learning how I write to different audiences
