# Global Managers

# I Used to Lose Half My Day to Time Zones

I'm a manager running a team that lives in three time zones — Singapore, Berlin, San Francisco. By the time I open Slack at 9 AM in San Francisco, my Singapore teammates have already finished their day, my Berlin team is two hours from lunch, and overnight pings from Tokyo are piled in three different inboxes.

I used to start every morning the same way: open Gmail, open Slack, open Lark, scroll for 30 minutes trying to figure out what I missed, and only then start the actual work. By the time I caught up, half the morning was gone. And worse — the act of catching up generated more messages ("sorry, just saw this"), which the team then had to triage on top of their own work.

I wasn't short on discipline. I was short on a way to hold three time zones in my head at the same time.

Then I tried OpenLoomi.

I didn't want another chatbot that waits for me to ask it something. I wanted an AI assistant that genuinely fits a manager's day across time zones: it polls every channel continuously, organizes the overnight signal for me, and only finds me when there's a decision I genuinely need to make.

---

## Problem 1: The APAC / EMEA gap ate my mornings

The painful part of being a global manager isn't the late-night Slack pings — it's the morning archaeology to figure out which of them actually matter. The exchange used to go like this:

- Open Slack and scroll the unread list, channel by channel
- Try to remember which of the 40 unread messages were just chat and which needed a reply
- Open Gmail and check if anything landed overnight from investors or partners
- Open Lark and check if the Berlin team left decisions in threads
- Stitch the morning's mental picture together from memory

If the night was busy, this got pushed to 10 AM, sometimes 11. By the time I was caught up, the Singapore team was already past lunch and Berlin was on their way out.

I realized this is exactly what AI should be doing. The actual hard part of the morning routine isn't the decisions — it's gathering the scattered overnight signal and reorganizing it into something a manager can act on.

So I connected my three primary channels in OpenLoomi:

```
use openloomi-connectors to connect Gmail
use openloomi-connectors to connect Slack
use openloomi-connectors to connect Lark
```

Gmail and Slack live in Composio's app catalog; the seven native Loomi connectors (Telegram / WhatsApp / iMessage / Feishu / DingTalk / QQ / WeChat) don't need Composio at all. You can mix native and Composio-managed accounts freely.

Verify with `list-accounts` — once every platform shows `active`, Loop picks them up on the next tick.

## Problem 2: 9 AM was a triage ritual, not a start of day

Once the connectors were in, the next thing I configured was the **Brief**. Every day at 9 AM (configurable in **Settings → Loop**), Loop gathers my open decision cards, today's calendar, and the contacts Loop knows I'll interact with today, and lays them out as a single page.

What lands in the Brief isn't a hardcoded count of "3 items" — it's everything Loop judged the previous day and overnight that genuinely needs my attention. If nothing needs me, the Brief says so.

The Brief isn't the only thing that runs on a schedule. **Throughout the day**, Loop ticks every 10 minutes and surfaces typed decision cards as new signals clear my gates:

- ✉️ **`email_reply`** — inbound from a contact on my important list with an action word, past my reply window
- 💬 **`im_reply`** — DM from a connected messaging app not yet responded to
- 📅 **`rsvp`** — calendar invite past 24 hours unanswered

Each card carries the draft, a reasoning trace, the confidence score, and the four-gesture flow — **Approve / Edit Draft / Later / Skip**. The dry-run is always shown before any outbound action — Loop never auto-sends.

By the time I open my laptop, the overnight queue is already filtered. I read the Brief, tap **Approve** on the two or three things that genuinely need me, and start the day in 5 minutes instead of 45.

## Problem 3: 6 PM was a guilt trip

The mirror problem to the morning Brief was the evening Wrap. Used to be: I'd log off, lie in bed, and think _"did I reply to that Singapore thread? Did I confirm the Berlin call? Did I acknowledge the partner's note?"_ If I was tired, I'd open Slack one more time to check, and that one more time would turn into 30 minutes of "while I'm here..."

Loop's **Wrap** at 6 PM (configurable in **Settings → Loop**) lists what Loop actually handled during the day — emails sent, RSVPs logged, replies posted — and what got parked for tomorrow. The goal is a clean mental inbox before I log off.

## What I really wanted to solve isn't a few automation tasks

At first I just wanted to save the 45 minutes every morning I was spending on overnight archaeology. But after a few weeks, I realized the real problem isn't that I'm missing a few automation scripts — it's that global-team work is fundamentally a time-zone information problem:

- The team lives in three time zones; my working day only covers one of them
- Async handoffs mean yesterday's decisions are buried in threads I haven't seen
- The cost of context-loading a 200-message overnight Slack scroll is real
- By the time I notice something, it's already 4–6 hours old in the other time zone

In the past, all this information had to be found and pieced together by me actively searching. The change OpenLoomi brings is inverting that relationship: instead of me waking up and trying to catch up, AI keeps watching every channel continuously and comes to me with the filtered list at the right moment.

- If a Tokyo teammate DMs at 11 PM their time, the `im_reply` card lands in my Brief already knowing who they are.
- If a partner emails overnight, the `email_reply` card surfaces with the draft grounded in our last conversation.
- If a calendar invite sits unanswered past 24 hours, the `rsvp` card waits in the morning's queue.
- If a public signal changes a contact's context, the `contact_update` card refreshes memory overnight.

I only need to focus on the parts that genuinely need my judgement — the actual reply, the RSVP, the decision.

## From "morning triage" to a continuously running manager loop

To make this work, I configured two kinds of working patterns in OpenLoomi.

**One is event-driven:**

| Trigger                                                | What happens                                                                       |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Inbound email / IM past reply window                   | `email_reply` or `im_reply` card with draft grounded in memory and reasoning trace |
| Calendar invite past 24 hours unanswered               | `rsvp` card with the response options                                              |
| Public signal on a contact (promotion, new role, etc.) | `contact_update` card proposes the memory refresh                                  |

**The other is scheduled:**

| Task                      | Schedule          | Purpose                                                          |
| ------------------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Today's To-Do (Brief)** | Every day at 9 AM | Pulls today's calendar + open decision cards + relevant contacts |
| **Today's Done (Wrap)**   | Every day at 6 PM | Lists what Loop handled for you today + what got parked          |

Together they form a loop that runs continuously across every time zone:

```
Continuously watch → Organize contact context → Propose action or draft → Human decides → Send and remember
```

Loop never sleeps. Brief and Wrap are the bookends; the queue runs in between. Compared to configuring an isolated per-channel workflow, I'd rather have OpenLoomi keep understanding _"what landed across all my time zones since I last looked."_

> 💡 **Tune what surfaces.** Global managers often have more signals than time. Three dials help: **Trusted contacts** (the auto-promote list, default ≥ 3 appearances in memory), **Sources** (toggle Email / Calendar / GitHub / Slack / Linear individually in **Settings → Loop → Sources**), and **Quiet hours** (pause windows around deep-work blocks in **Settings → Attention Agent**).

## How to set this up

Three connector calls is all it takes. The same commands work in OpenLoomi Desktop, the Claude plugin, or the Codex plugin — invoke `openloomi-connectors` in chat, once per channel:

```
use openloomi-connectors to connect Gmail
use openloomi-connectors to connect Slack
use openloomi-connectors to connect Lark
```

Verify with `list-accounts` — once every platform shows `active`, Loop picks them up on the next tick.

From that moment on:

- Every 10 min Loop polls all your channels and surfaces typed cards as signals clear your gates
- The Brief lands at 9 AM (or your configured time); the Wrap lands at 6 PM
- Memory auto-promotes frequent contacts into your trusted list
- Loomi bubbles the high-confidence cards in the corner of your screen during the day

Tune trusted contacts, source toggles, and brief/wrap times in **Settings → Loop**. Loomi's bubble cadence, quiet hours, and theme are in **Settings → Attention Agent**. Want the Brief in a different time zone or only in certain languages? Set the time in **Settings → Loop → Morning briefing time**; signal prioritisation respects the Interests panel in **Settings → Personalization**.

OpenLoomi's audit log records every execution — how many times Loop has polled, which cards surfaced, which Approves I tapped, which drafts I edited. This way I can hand the overnight triage off to AI and still see exactly what it did.

## What I need isn't another chatbot

This experience reinforced something for me: an AI assistant for a global manager shouldn't be a chat window waiting for me to ask.

If every interaction still requires me to open a tool, type a question, and supply the context, then the responsibility for gathering information and pushing work forward still sits on my shoulders.

What I actually need is an AI that can keep working around the time-zone gap:

- It watches the channels that matter — Gmail, Slack, Lark, the rest of my stack
- It keeps spotting the inbound that needs a reply, even when I'm asleep
- It proactively organizes the contact context across time zones
- It drafts the reply grounded in what I and the contact have already discussed
- It hands the send button back to me when judgement is needed
- It remembers my edits and choices, gradually learning how I prioritize across regions
