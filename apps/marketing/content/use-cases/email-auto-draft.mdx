# Email Auto-Fetch + Context-Aware Draft Generation

## Never Re-Read a Thread Just to Draft a Reply Again

Professionals spend an average of 2–3 hours per day on email — much of it re-reading ongoing conversations to recall context before typing a response. Most AI email tools either auto-reply blindly (low accuracy) or require you to manually paste context every time (high friction). OpenLoomi solves both: it auto-fetches your emails, understands the full conversation context, and generates accurate draft replies that you approve in seconds.

**What it does:**

| Step           | Action                                                                                         |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 📥 Auto-Fetch  | Pull new emails from your Gmail or email account at a set schedule                             |
| 🧠 Context Map | Analyze the full conversation thread — identify topics, decisions, and pending items           |
| ✍️ Draft       | Generate a context-aware reply draft tailored to the conversation and your communication style |
| ✅ One-Click   | Review and send, or edit before sending                                                        |

## Why This Works

**🧠 Context, Not Just Content**

Most AI email tools dump the entire email thread into the model — expensive and often counterproductive. OpenLoomi builds a compact context map first: what was decided, what's still open, what the sender actually needs. The draft is generated from that map, not raw text.

**⚡ Token Efficiency = Speed + Accuracy**

By condensing threads into structured context summaries before drafting, OpenLoomi uses roughly 40–60% fewer tokens than naive approaches. Less noise means faster generation and higher accuracy — drafts land closer to what you'd actually write.

**✅ Human in the Loop, Every Time**

Auto-send email AI tools are a liability. OpenLoomi generates drafts you own — review in seconds, send with confidence. You set the bar, not the algorithm.
