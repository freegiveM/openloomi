# Attention Agent

> At 9 AM, the moment you sit down at your desk, before you've even opened your inbox, a small fox (Loomi) peeks out in the bottom-right corner of your screen with a bubble that reads: "Sarah's meeting invite still needs an RSVP — due today at 11."
>
> That's the Attention Agent — the always-on desktop companion built into OpenLoomi. It doesn't fight for your attention; it nudges you, gently, at the moments that matter.

The Attention Agent is a transparent little fox named **Loomi** that lives on top of your desktop. 168×168, always-on-top, with its own expression animations. It proactively tells you "what's waiting for you today", but it never interrupts what you're doing. Close the main OpenLoomi window and Loomi doesn't go away — it retreats into the system tray and comes back the moment you tap it.

## Overview

The Attention Agent is not a chat window, not a toolbar, and not a notification centre. It's more like a partner at your desk — appearing only in the few key moments when you actually need a reminder:

- 🔔 **A new email arrives — but only the one that truly needs your reply**
- ⏰ **A calendar event slides past your usual response window**
- 📋 **At 9 AM sharp, today's to-do glides gently into your view**
- ✅ **At 6 PM, you get a recap of what was handled for you today**
- ❓ **You asked the main window something with a long answer — Loomi pops a one-line summary first**

The principle: **never intrusive, only present in the right instant.**

## When Loomi Shows Up

### ☀️ 9 AM — Today's To-Do

Every morning at 9 AM, Loomi pulls today's items from your email, calendar, Linear, Slack, and code-review queue, ranks them by priority, and slides a small card into the corner of your screen. A glance is enough to know what's ahead.

The actual ranking and decision-card generation comes from [Loop](/docs/loop); the Attention Agent is just the messenger.

### ✉️ Incoming email — only the ones that need you

Not every email triggers a bubble. Loomi only pops up when **all three** of these hold:

- The sender is on the "important contacts" list in [OpenLoomi Memory](/docs/memory)
- The subject or body contains an action word — _"RSVP"_, _"please confirm"_, _"reply"_, _"get back to me"_
- The email has passed your usual reply window (default 4 hours) and is still unanswered

Which platforms Loomi reads from depends on your [Connectors](/docs/connectors) — toggle a connector off and the Attention Agent stops seeing those signals.

### 📅 Calendar invites past their RSVP window

When a meeting invite has been sitting unanswered for over 24 hours, Loomi sways gently at your desk until you respond. Tap the bubble to accept, decline, or propose a new time without ever opening your calendar app.

### 🌙 6 PM — Today's Done

Every evening at 6 PM, Loomi delivers a recap of what [Loop](/docs/loop) handled for you during the day — emails sent, RSVPs logged, Linear tickets closed. The goal: a clean mental inbox before you log off.

### 🪟 When the main window is closed

Close the main OpenLoomi window and Loomi doesn't disappear. It shrinks into the system tray (Windows / Linux) or the menu bar (macOS), still watching the door for new reminders.

## Gestures

Loomi isn't a one-way notifier. It responds to a handful of gestures:

<img
  src="/img/openloomi/loomi-pet.gif"
  alt="Loomi pet animations — idle, presenting, sleeping and other gestures"
/>

| Gesture                | How                               | What Happens                                                                                         |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tap a bubble**       | Click the bubble next to Loomi    | Opens the matching detail view — a [Chat](/docs/chat) thread for emails, a calendar card for invites |
| **Drag Loomi**         | Drag Loomi anywhere on screen     | Loomi remembers the spot and stays there next time                                                   |
| **Right-click Loomi**  | Right-click for the context menu  | Pause reminders · Check now · Open main window · Quit                                                |
| **Double-click Loomi** | Double-click                      | Summons the main OpenLoomi window directly                                                           |
| **Reply to it**        | Tap a bubble → _"I'll handle it"_ | The main window pre-fills a reply draft; you just confirm                                            |

If you don't want to be interrupted in a moment — say, during a demo — right-click and pick **Pause reminders for 1 hour**. Loomi quiets down and slips on a little sleep-mask animation. You can also set recurring quiet hours (e.g. evenings, weekends) in **Settings → Attention Agent**.

## 🃏 Decision Cards You Can See

Loomi bubbles whatever Loop decides. Here's the full catalog of card types currently shipping — built-in types plus two representative custom examples — and what each one looks like when it lands in your queue. Every card arrives with the same four-button tray (Approve / Edit Draft / Later / Skip); only the type, label, and confidence differ.

## Custom Decision Types & Signal Sources

<img
  src="/img/openloomi/loop/birthday-wish-card.png"
  alt="A custom birthday_wish decision card — routed by the force_birthday_today classifier rule with the cake icon"
/>

Loomi is the messenger for [Loop](/docs/loop), so anything Loop can decide, Loomi can surface — including the **custom decision types and custom signal channels** you register yourself. If you don't know what those are yet, read the [Extending Loop section](/docs/loop#extending-loop-custom-types--channels) first; the short version is that you can teach Loop new kinds of cards (e.g. a `birthday_wish` type for birthday greetings) and new sources of signals (e.g. Stripe charges) without writing any code.

### How a custom type becomes a bubble

When you register a custom decision type, three things change at the next tick:

1. **The classifier sees it.** Loop's prompt now lists your custom ids (e.g. `birthday_wish`) alongside the built-ins, with the `actionKind` each one maps to. The agent can therefore pick them the same way it picks `email_reply` or `im_reply`.
2. **The card UI renders it.** Decision cards in the main OpenLoomi window, in the pet card, and in the bubble that Loomi shows use the `label` and `icon` you provided — so a `birthday_wish` card arrives with `ri-cake-2-line` and the text **"Birthday wish"** instead of falling back to a generic icon.
3. **The bubble tone adapts.** When Loomi shows a one-liner summary for that card, it pulls from your custom `label` rather than a hard-coded string. The same applies to the chip Loomi tints on the card list.

So once the type is registered, there's nothing else to configure for Loomi — it picks up the extension the same way the rest of Loop does.

### How a custom channel feeds Loomi

Registering a custom channel adds a new **source** of signals on top of the five built-ins (Email / Calendar / GitHub / Slack / Linear). The signal flow is unchanged — the watcher pulls, the classifier judges, the queue holds the cards, Loomi surfaces them — but the source list is now yours to extend.

Concretely, the next tick will:

- Throttle-poll the channel on its own `pollIntervalSec` (default 600 s, minimum 60 s) using Composio under the hood.
- Stamp every record with the channel's `signalType` (e.g. `stripe_charge`) so the classifier and the audit log know which source produced it.
- Inject a "User-defined channels" block into the tick prompt describing the `payloadShape` — so the agent knows what `stripe_charge` records look like and can prioritise them correctly.

From Loomi's perspective, a `stripe_charge` decision card and a `email_reply` decision card are the same kind of thing — both become a bubble with the same gestures, the same Approve / Edit Draft / Later / Skip actions, and the same audit-trail treatment.

> 💡 **Loomi stays out of the extension API.** The `openloomi-loop` skill talks to Loop's local JSON registries (`~/.openloomi/loop/custom-types.json` and `custom-channels.json`); Loomi just observes. You never need to teach the pet anything — it inherits.

### How classifier rules pin what Loomi surfaces

Beyond teaching Loop **new kinds** of cards and **new sources** of signals, you can also pin the routing for specific signal patterns — so the LLM can't drift and accidentally surface a generic `email_reply` card when you really wanted a `birthday_wish` card. That's what [classifier rules](/docs/loop#register-a-deterministic-classifier-rule) do. A rule is a tiny safe AST of field predicates (`signal.type eq "contact_birthday"` and `signal.payload.daysUntilNext eq 0`, for example) plus a `then` block that forces `type` / `actionKind` / a confidence floor.

Because the rule is enforced **twice** (in the prompt the agent sees _and_ in a server-side post-processor that rewrites the persisted decision), Loomi ends up bubbling the card you actually wanted. A typical example:

| Without a rule                                                                                         | With a rule                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Same-day birthday signal → LLM classifies as `email_reply` (generic) → Loomi shows an email-reply card | Same signal → rule pins `type=birthday_wish`, `actionKind=email_reply`, `confidence ≥ 0.9` → Loomi shows a `🎂 Birthday wish` card with your custom icon |

A rule with `then.type = "noop"` does the opposite — it **suppresses** the decision entirely so the bubble never appears for that pattern. Rules live in `~/.openloomi/loop/classifier-rules.json`, are registered via the same [`openloomi-loop`](/docs/skills#openloomi-loop) skill (or the `PUT /api/loop/classifier-rules` HTTP endpoint), and apply on the very next tick — no rebuild needed. There's also a `POST /api/loop/classifier-rules/dry-run` endpoint that previews which rules would fire on a given signal, so you can iterate on the AST before going live.

## How to Find It

The Attention Agent is on by default. After installing the OpenLoomi desktop app:

1. **First launch** — Loomi walks out from the bottom-right corner and introduces itself.
2. **Can't find it?** — Click the OpenLoomi icon in the system tray (Windows / Linux) or menu bar (macOS), then pick **Show Attention Agent**.
3. **Want to turn it off?** — Right-click Loomi → Quit, or open the main OpenLoomi window → **Settings → Attention Agent**.

> 💡 Loomi is **transparent** by default — it doesn't get in the way of your work. If you'd like it more visible, the settings panel lets you add a faint background, adjust the scale (default 168×168), or pin it above full-screen apps.

## Tips

- **Let Loomi sit on a second monitor.** If you use a primary display for focus work, dragging Loomi to your secondary screen gives you reminders without breaking your flow.
- **Tune the quiet hours.** Schedule pause windows around deep-work blocks, recurring meetings, or after-hours — so Loomi only nudges you when you're actually available to act.
- **Pair it with Loop.** Loomi alone is a polite messenger; pair it with [Loop](/docs/loop) and you get the full proactive desk — Loop decides what matters, Loomi makes sure you actually see it.
- **Trust the memory layer.** Loomi's "important contacts" list comes from [OpenLoomi Memory](/docs/memory). The more you use OpenLoomi, the smarter the filter gets — give it a week before you tune anything.

## FAQ

### Does Loomi watch everything I do?

No. Loomi only reads signals tied to you from the **platforms you've explicitly connected** (email, calendar, chat). It does not read your screen, keyboard, or clipboard. See [Privacy & Security](/docs/privacy-security) for the full breakdown of what the Attention Agent does and doesn't see.

### Can I have it remind me of only certain things?

Yes. In the main OpenLoomi window → **Settings → Attention Agent**, you can toggle each reminder source independently:

- Email reminders (the ones that need a reply)
- Calendar reminders (overdue RSVP)
- Today's to-do (every day at 9 AM)
- Today's done (every day at 6 PM)

Each can have its own schedule or be turned off entirely.

### Does it eat up resources?

The Attention Agent is lightweight — just a 168×168 transparent window, no continuous network calls. All the heavy lifting ("scan email / calendar") runs in OpenLoomi's background; Loomi's only job is to surface the result at the right moment.

### Can I use it across multiple screens?

Yes. Loomi defaults to the bottom-right of your primary monitor, but wherever you drag it — which monitor, which corner — it remembers.

### Can I switch the pet's look?

Yes. Loomi ships with two built-in themes (`fox` and `capybara`) and a fully file-driven customization layer:

- **Switch theme** — right-click Loomi → `Theme → Capybara` (or `Fox`).
- **Custom themes** — drop a folder at `~/.openloomi/pet-custom/<name>/` with PNGs named `idle.png`, `thinking.png`, `presenting.png`, etc. Loomi auto-discovers them and they appear in the theme picker.
- **Per-state overrides** — edit `~/.openloomi/pet-config.json` to point individual states at any PNG on disk:

  ```json
  {
    "version": 1,
    "activeTheme": "capybara",
    "customThemesDir": "~/.openloomi/pet-custom",
    "overrides": {
      "fox": { "idle": "/Users/me/Pictures/my-fox-idle.png" }
    }
  }
  ```

- **External edits auto-apply** — the config watcher listens for changes and re-paints within ~250 ms; no app restart needed.
- **`presenting` state** — when a decision moves to `done` and you haven't reviewed it yet, Loomi shows the `presenting` sprite + bubble ("All wrapped up — ready when you are"). Click the bubble to flip back to `happy`.

### What's the difference between the Attention Agent and Loop?

- **The Attention Agent** is the **little pet on your desktop**, gently reminding you in your line of sight.
- **Loop** is the **proactive execution engine** in the background, picking up scattered signals and turning into decision cards waiting for your approval.

The two work as a pair: the "Today's To-Do" and "Today's Done" that Loomi pushes are essentially Loop's output, surfaced to your eyes by the Attention Agent. To see how that pipeline actually works, read [Loop](/docs/loop) next.

### Do custom Loop types & channels show up in the bubbles?

Yes. When you register a [custom decision type](/docs/loop#extending-loop-custom-types--channels) or a [custom channel](/docs/loop#extending-loop-custom-types--channels) (e.g. a `birthday_wish` type or a Stripe charges source), Loomi picks them up on the very next tick — same bubble, same Approve / Edit Draft / Later / Skip actions, but with your custom `label` and `icon` rendered on the card and in the bubble summary. See the [Custom Decision Types & Signal Sources section](#custom-decision-types--signal-sources) above for the full flow.

### Can I force specific signal patterns to always become a specific card?

Yes — register a [classifier rule](/docs/loop#register-a-deterministic-classifier-rule) and Loop will pin the routing for that pattern. The card Loomi bubbles is then deterministic, regardless of how the underlying LLM interprets the signal. Rules support a `noop` action too, which suppresses the decision so the bubble never appears for that pattern. See the [How classifier rules pin what Loomi surfaces](#how-classifier-rules-pin-what-loomi-surfaces) section above for the example and the safe-AST predicate syntax.
